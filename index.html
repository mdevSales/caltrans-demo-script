"This is the Grant Program Fit Playbook — a guided flow native to the Opportunity record. Think of it as the structured 5-to-7-minute conversation Sarah has with the applicant to assess program fit, project readiness, and next steps. Nine short steps."

"What's on this flow:"

  • Context — confirm grant program, applicant type, funding request, geography (pre-filled from the record)
  • Program Guidance — a program-specific refresher (TIRCP, ATP, Clean California, etc.) so the rep goes in briefed
  • Discovery — the challenge, beneficiaries, sponsor, timeline, prior-application history
  • Program Alignment — applicant/category eligibility, geographic scope, priority alignment, public benefit
  • Project Readiness — scope, budget, schedule, plan inclusion, partners, community engagement, delivery risks, admin capacity
  • Match-Funding Readiness — required? confirmed? in progress?
  • Technical Assistance Needs — multi-select where the applicant needs help
  • Recommendation — the flow computes Program Fit, Readiness score (0-6), Risk level, Primary Risk, and a Recommended Next Step in real time. Rep can accept or override.
  • Follow-Up Actions — pick from schedule readiness session, send materials, refer to TA, eligibility review, draft email, etc.

"Every step is grounded in the real Caltrans grants process — the fields, the eligibility questions, the program-specific priorities. It's not a generic sales stage checklist."

"And this one writes back. When Sarah clicks Finish, twelve assessment fields on the Opportunity get populated — recommendation, readiness score, risk level, primary risk, match readiness, TA needs, full summary — plus a follow-up Task with all the actions she selected."
